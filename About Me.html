Values and Beliefs

I value Collaboration and want my classes to be one filled with teamwork and self-motivated learning where creatvity is fostered through communication, collaboration and open-mindness where a lack of judgement by ALL parties within the classroom. There is not such thing as a bad idea or bad question, its just an idea or a question

I want their to be a commitment to learning for both teachers and students alike. I want students to feel secure and to know I am there to support them, helping to build a sense of belonging

With these values above my classroom will be a place of excellence where students strive for perfection but in a way where they learn from their mistakes and be held accountable

These values have been implemented at each teaching opportunity across my last three placements for more informatiion

My Teaching Philosphy

By honoring the values of secuirty and belonging along with an open-minded attitude the learnes in my classroom can feel as if they belong in an inclusive learning environment. I want my teaching to centre around inquiry-based learning or opportunities for students to problem solve and explore real-world scenarios. As mentioned previously I value group work however I also appreciate the importance of testing individuals within the “group” to see what “exactly” they know. This is important because “to teach is to know the individual and their learning style and needs”. The theorists which inform my learning style is Vygotsky, students learning through social interaction and mimicking as well as Piaget's cognitive development stages and B.F skinner's positive reinforcement studies. For me to know if the students have learnt the content I usually write questions as part of a conclusion or “exit ticket”. Worksheets are also a wonderful way to assess learning. In conclusion, I always try my best to use my beliefs to inform my teaching practise.
